This blog is the engineering notebook of SHUKE‑LABS. Product pages show what we built; this space explains why we built it that way.

The most useful part of engineering is the decisions—the tradeoffs, the wrong turns, and the changes of mind. Here you’ll find two kinds of pieces: chronicles (how a project came to be) and thesis pieces (one design choice argued with commits and data). Expect first-person reasoning, not polished marketing. If a piece changed my mind halfway through, you’ll read that too.

The aim is practical: durable reasoning that helps future decisions, not feature PR spin. Welcome to the workbench.
